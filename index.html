The problem

Three layers.
No one watching all of them.

GPU metrics, network logs, framework traces — three dashboards, no connection between them. Every failure turns into a manual investigation. The culprit is almost always a cross-layer interaction that none of your existing tools can see.

  • 01
    Failures get misattributed 70–80% of apparent GPU hardware failures are actually framework or network issues. Teams replace hardware that isn't broken, restart runs that were minutes from finishing.
  • 02
    Inference degrades silently Throughput drops. Latency climbs. GPU utilization stays green. Your cluster looks fine while users notice the difference.
  • 03
    Debugging costs hours A distributed training failure — NCCL timeout, rank hang, network flap — takes 2–6 hours to root-cause without cross-stack visibility. That's an expensive run wasted and a team pulled off other work.

Inference reliability

Your cluster looks healthy.
Your users disagree.

Training failures are loud — jobs crash, teams get paged. Inference failures are quiet. Throughput drops. P99 climbs. GPU utilization stays green. Harbor surfaces what your dashboards miss.

KV cache visibility
Cache exhaustion is a common source of silent degradation that's invisible without framework-layer instrumentation. Harbor tracks hit rates, eviction pressure, and sizing mismatches against your actual traffic.

TTFT + throughput baselines
Harbor maintains rolling baselines for time-to-first-token, inter-token latency, and tokens per second — and alerts on drift before your users notice. P99 degradation caught at the source, not in a support ticket.

Hardware → latency correlation
A GPU thermal throttle doesn't crash anything. It just silently cuts compute throughput, showing up as a latency bump two minutes later. Harbor correlates hardware events to serving latency with sub-second precision.

How it works

Prevent. Diagnose. Remediate.

01 / PREVENT

Pre-deployment validation

Harbor runs cross-stack health checks before any training run or inference deployment starts. Catch topology errors, bandwidth gaps, and misconfigured KV cache sizing before they cost you a 6-hour job.

02 / DIAGNOSE

Live cross-stack monitoring

During training and inference, Harbor correlates hardware, network, and framework signals in real time. When something goes wrong, you get a causal chain — not a wall of disconnected logs.

03 / REMEDIATE

Actionable remediation

Harbor tells you what failed and what to do about it. Operator-level guidance a software-focused ML engineer can act on — no hardware background required. Automated remediation on the roadmap.
